Distance Metrics ↓¶

1.¶

Explain why computing the proximity between two attributes is often simpler than computing the similarity between two objects.

→ Calculating two attributes is generally simpler, as data transformation is not necessary to obtain the expected results, unlike calculating two objects that generally need to undergo a binary transformation in order to be calculated in an equivalent way.

2.¶

The Euclidean distance works well when we are working in a continuous space and we can move in a "straight line" from one point to the other. This is the case of flying between two airports if we ignore traffic restrictions and the roundness of the earth. When is Manhattan distance preferable? Think of two examples and justify.

It is preferable to use the Manhattan distance whenever the points are organized in the form of a large one and the expected result is the distance between the items analyzed in relation to the grid, with no geometric distance being necessary. A good example is the distance between two rival chess pieces or the distance from a bus stop to a house a few blocks away. [link de referência]

→ Bus 1 as X_1 and bus 2 as X_2 with the time of arrival start in 0 and compute them with Euclidean distance, as there are continuous numerical variables and it is intended to identify the absolute distance, which takes into account all variables and does not remove redundancies.

5.¶

Explain the difference between the distance calculated using 'simple matching' and the 'Jaccard' distance. In what situation, we apply one or the other?

→ While the distance calculated as simple correspondence is used to dentify the proximity of two elements, the Jaccard distance is used to measure the similarity and diversity of sets of finite samples and is defined as the size of the intersection divided by the size of the joint of the sets sample.

Data Visualization¶

1.¶

Figure 1 shows a “scatterplot”. What information can you infer from this graph?

Volcano data:

  • Expected time
  • Eruption duration
  • In the graph we see that the data are distributed in a positive correlation, in two distinct groups between the shortest duration of the eruption with the shortest expected times and the longest duration with the longest expected times.

2.¶

Figure 2 shows a “parallel plot”. What kind of information can you infer from this graph?

Traffic data

  • Dead drivers
  • Distance between vehicles
  • Petrol Price
  • Security law
  • The data are related by means of horizontal lines between the vertical columns, where we can see that people who use the feeling of security, are more likely to pay more for gasoline, maintain a greater distance between vehicles, resulting in a reduction in the number of deaths per transit assistant.
